the goal of the project¶

  • The goal of this project to play detective, is to detect persons that might be responsible for enron's corrutption based on the emails communication among stuff, and other financial data such as salary, bonus, etc.

how machine learning is useful in this problem¶

  • This will be a supervised classification problem.
  • "Supervise" means there will be a bunch of samples already with labels, these somes alreay have x(features) and y(labels). Based on this, when new x(features) come in, how accurately can we predict y(label)?
  • "classification" means the y(labels) will be discrete, in this case, it is yes or no, yes means the person is responsible for the corruption, and no means the person is not responsible.

process used to pick features¶

  • I used ExtraTreesClassifier to get feature importance on both scaled and data and unscaled data, the top features lists have over laps, I choose the top 5 features that appeared on either list.

feature scaling¶

  • I did feature scaling, as the numeric feature show large difference in range. For email related features, they are in tens or hundreds, but for finance related features, they are much bigger than that. For algorithm that are based on distance metrics, such as KNN, feature scaling is crutial.

feature engineering¶

  • I created new feature bonus_salary_ratio
  • The rational behind it is that: I thought people who are responsible for the company's corruption might be the one who is the top person, who can normally get more bonus. but normally the bonus should be in proportion with someone's salary. If the bonus_salary_ratio is out of range, that means that person could have super power in the company, and be responsible for the company's corruption.

Parameter tuning is like choosing a good spot between bias and variance, which can lead to best out sample accuracy. Question5¶

validation and its importance¶

Answer:

  • validation: model validation is the process of evaluting a trained model a testing data set. If no validation is used, the model might suffer from overfitting, as we keep training the model, and only focus on reducing trainning error, actually, the out sample error might increase in which case, overfitting occurs.

  • Normally, if the data set is huge, 70% of data can be used for training, and 30% of data are used for validation. Using data that is not included in the training for valiation, in this way, we can see not only how the alogirhtm is doing on the in sample data, but also see how the algorith is doing on the out sample data, which are actually more important. Because we are more interested in seeing how the alogirhtm is doing in the future on the unseen data.

  • For for this small dataset, with 140+ observations, Stratified ShuffleSplit cross-validator in sklearn is used, it returns stratified randomized folds. The folds are made by preserving the percentage of samples for each class, in this case poi and non-poi. When using cross validation, say 10 fold-cross validation, the samples are divided into 10 pieces, we run 10 rounds, in each round, each piece got to be the test samples, and the remaning nice pieces are the traning samples, then after ten rounds, we pool all the training results together, and pool all the test results toghter. This kind of cross validation if very useful for small datasets, as in this way, it is almost seems like we have increased our samples by 10 folds if we use 10 folds cross validation. The stratified cross validation codes are in test.py file, which is out side of poi_id.py file.

interpretation of precision and recall¶

  • precision = 1.0*true_positives/(true_positives+false_positives)

  • recall = 1.0*true_positives/(true_positives+false_negatives)

  • precision is the correct positive ratio, that is of all the samples that the alogirthm recognize as positive, the percentage of true positive.

  • recall is the rate of recognizing the sample as positive, when all the samples are actually positive.
